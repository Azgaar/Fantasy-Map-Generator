# Azgaar's Fantasy Map Generator v1.123.0: Eroded Terrain and Satellite Texture

The 3D scene mode got two new features: **Eroded Terrain** and **Satellite Texture**.
Both are opt-in, view-only: your map data is never changed, this is purely about how it's displayed in 3D.

You'll find both as new checkboxes in **3D mode settings** (press `O` while in 3D scene).

---

## Eroded Terrain

Before (classic mesh) vs. after (Eroded Terrain enabled):

![No erosion](screenshots/no%20erosion.jpeg)
![Eroded terrain](screenshots/eroded%20terrain.jpeg)

Normally, the 3D mesh has one vertex per heightmap cell – fine for a quick preview, but it
looks blocky and too smooth, with no ridges, gullies, or carved valleys.

With **Eroded Terrain** enabled, the generator bakes a much higher-resolution heightmap on
the GPU and builds a dense mesh from it, adding:

- Ridgelines and crests on hills and mountains
- Gully and drainage detail on slopes
- River valleys carved into the terrain
- Coastlines and cliffs that follow your map's true coastline shape, not the grid

The wireframe view shows just how much more geometry is involved:

![No erosion, wireframe](screenshots/no%20erosion%20wireframe.jpeg)
![Eroded terrain, wireframe](screenshots/erosion%20wireframe.jpeg)

When you turn it on, a few new sliders appear:

| Setting            | What it does                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Mesh detail**    | How dense the new terrain mesh is. Higher = more detail, slower to bake and render. |
| **Gully strength** | How strong the ridge/gully erosion detail is.                                       |
| **River valleys**  | How deeply rivers carve into the terrain.                                           |
| **Detail octaves** | How many layers of noise detail are added. More = finer detail, slower bake.        |

The bake is cached, so changing the height-scale slider or re-opening the 3D view doesn't
re-bake from scratch. If your GPU can't handle the bake, the view falls back to the
classic mesh.

## Satellite Texture

![Satellite texture screenshot](screenshots/satellite%20texture.jpeg)

**Satellite Texture** replaces the normal map-style texture on the 3D terrain with a fully
procedural, satellite-photo-style look:

- Ground colors generated from your map's biomes and climate (forests, deserts, tundra, snowcaps, etc.)
- Slope-based shading – rock and cliffs on steep terrain, sand near shores
- Rivers, lakes, and coastlines drawn from your map's real geometry, including different
  colors for freshwater, salt, sinkhole, lava, and frozen lakes
- Animated water – ocean swell and shimmer, calm ripples on lakes, flowing rivers, and even
  waterfalls where rivers drop steeply

Satellite Texture is independent of Eroded Terrain – you can use either one on its own, or
combine both for the most detailed result. It's not available together with Wireframe mode.

A **Texture resolution** dropdown (512–8192 px) controls how sharp the generated texture is.

---

## How to try it

1. In **Layers**, switch to **3D scene** view mode.
2. Check **Erode terrain** and/or **Satellite texture**.

Both features are experimental and mostly just for fun, having no impact on your map data. Feedback on how the terrain and
textures look on your maps is very welcome!

## Credits

The Eroded Terrain technique is based on the noise-based erosion filter described in
[Fast and Gorgeous Erosion Filter](https://blog.runevision.com/2026/03/fast-and-gorgeous-erosion-filter.html)
by runevision, which builds on two Shadertoy shaders:

- [Eroded Terrain Noise](https://www.shadertoy.com/view/MtGcWh) by Clay John (2018)
- [Terrain Erosion Noise](https://www.shadertoy.com/view/7ljcRW) by Fewes / Felix Westin (2023)

Our implementation was written from scratch based on the published descriptions of the
technique: huge thanks to the authors for sharing their work!

If you're curious how erosion detail is generated, check out runevision's
[video on the technique](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4V21_uUK8Y). It's a great watch
and explains the idea far better than I can.
